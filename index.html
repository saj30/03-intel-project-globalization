Sustainability Through the Ages

Discover Intel's journey to a more sustainable future, from early innovation to today's climate action and green manufacturing.

1995

First Environmental Report

Intel publishes its first environmental, health, and safety report.

This milestone marked the beginning of Intel's public commitment to transparency and environmental responsibility.

2008

Green Power Leadership Award

Intel is recognized as the largest voluntary purchaser of green power in the U.S.

Intel won the EPA Green Power Leadership Award, highlighting its leadership in renewable energy use.

2012

Water Conservation

Intel begins large-scale water conservation projects.

These projects save billions of gallons of water each year, supporting Intel's sustainability goals.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches the RISE strategy.

RISE stands for Responsibility, Inclusion, Sustainability, and Enabling technology for good.

2021

100% Green Power in U.S., Europe, Israel

Intel achieves 100% renewable electricity use in key regions.

This achievement covers Intel's operations in the U.S., Europe, and Israel, reducing its carbon footprint.

2022

Net-Zero by 2040 Commitment

Intel commits to net-zero greenhouse gas emissions by 2040.

Intel aims to reach net-zero emissions across its global operations, building on years of environmental progress.

2023

Water Restoration

Intel restores 100% of its global freshwater use.

Billions of gallons are returned to local watersheds, supporting communities and ecosystems.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit.

The summit brings together leaders to advance green semiconductor manufacturing and share best practices.
